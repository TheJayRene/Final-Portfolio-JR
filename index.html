Introductions

What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects individuals with ovaries, typically during their reproductive years. It is characterized by irregular menstrual cycles, elevated levels of androgens (male hormones), and the presence of multiple small cysts on the ovaries. Common symptoms include acne, excessive hair growth, weight gain, and difficulty conceiving. PCOS can also increase the risk of long-term health issues like type 2 diabetes and heart disease.

What is HS? (Hidradenitis Suppurativa)

Hidradenitis Suppurativa (HS) is a chronic skin condition that causes painful, inflamed lumps to form under the skin, typically in areas where skin rubs together, such as the armpits, groin, buttocks, and breasts. These lumps can progress to abscesses and tunnels beneath the skin, often resulting in scarring.

What is Endometriosis??

Endometriosis is a medical condition where tissue similar to the lining of the uterus (endometrium) grows outside the uterus, often on the ovaries, fallopian tubes, and surrounding pelvic organs. This misplaced tissue can cause inflammation, severe pain (especially during menstruation), heavy periods, and, in some cases, infertility.
